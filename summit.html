Step 2 of 4 - Confidentiality Agreement

Confidentiality Agreement

Please read the following confidentiality requirements carefully.

You must agree to all terms and provide your digital signature to proceed.

Before you register, you need to understand what you're agreeing to.

This summit operates under "Traffic Light Protocol Red" - the highest level of confidentiality. Everything discussed in this meeting stays in the room where it's discussed. No exceptions.

What We'll Be Discussing

Frank conversations about:

  • The real financial situation of your campus store
  • Staffing levels, changes, and expertise needs
  • Operational challenges and solutions
  • Strategic decisions your institution is requiring

Who Can Attend

This meeting is designed for management-level staff who can speak candidly about their store's operations and strategic decisions.

If you are the primary CSC member: You can attend yourself or designate someone else from your team.

Important consideration about collective bargaining agreements: If you or your designee are covered by a collective bargaining agreement, you must review your contract to ensure there are no provisions that would require you to disclose information from this meeting. By registering, you assume full responsibility for any conflicts between this confidentiality agreement and your employment contract.

If you are designating someone else: You are personally responsible for ensuring they understand and will follow these rules. The CSC Board reserves the right to exclude any designee if they believe that person cannot contribute meaningfully to the conversation.

The Rules

In-Person: "What's said in the room stays in the room. No recording of any kind. No sharing notes or information afterward."

Virtual: You must be alone, camera on, microphone active throughout, no recording, no sharing information afterward.

Consequences

Breaking confidentiality results in immediate membership termination with no appeals or refunds.

Step 2 of 4 - Virtual Attendance Protocol

Virtual Attendance Protocol

Virtual attendance requires additional protocols to maintain confidentiality.

Please review and agree to the following requirements:

Core Requirements

Before attending: Test camera/microphone, ensure stable internet, secure a private lockable room, and silence notifications.

During the meeting: Your camera needs to stay on for the entire meeting. Microphone should remain active throughout. You cannot have others in the room, record sessions, take screenshots, use speakers that risk audio leakage, or join from shared spaces.

If Technical Problems Occur

Reconnect quickly when connection drops. If camera/microphone fails persistently, disconnect and troubleshoot. If someone unexpectedly enters your space, immediately leave the call and rejoin once secured.

Guiding Principle

Virtual participants join "the room with us, just through a screen." The summit depends on confidentiality for candid discussions about financial and staffing challenges. You must protect conversations with the same security commitment as in-person participants.

Contact for Questions

If you have setup concerns, reach out to Greg McPherson (greg@campusstores.ca) before the meeting.
